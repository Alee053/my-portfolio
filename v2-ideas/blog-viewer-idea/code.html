DECONSTRUCTING NEURAL-ODE BRIDGE

[DATE: 2024.11.05] | [TAGS: ML | RESEARCH | DYNAMICS] | [STATUS: ARCHIVED]
FIG_01: LATENT_SPACE_PROJECTION.EXE

The integration of Neural Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) represents a paradigm shift in continuous-depth models. By bridging the gap between discrete residual networks and continuous dynamical systems, we achieve higher parameter efficiency and natural handling of irregularly sampled data.

In this framework, instead of specifying a discrete sequence of hidden layers:
h_{t+1} = h_t + f(h_t, θ_t)

We define the derivative of the hidden state using a neural network:
dh(t)/dt = f(h(t), t, θ) 

+-----------+       +-----------+       +-----------+
|  INPUT X  | ----> |  ODE SOLV | ----> | OUTPUT Y  |
+-----------+       +-----------+       +-----------+
                          ^
                          |
                  +-------+-------+
                  |  f(h, t, θ)   |
                  |  NEURAL NET   |
                  +---------------+

Architecture: Continuous Flow Pipeline

The solver evaluates the function f at multiple time steps to compute the final state. This allows for a flexible trade-off between computation time and accuracy at inference time. During training, the adjoint sensitivity method enables backpropagation through the ODE solver without storing intermediate activations, leading to constant memory cost as a function of depth.

"The mapping between discrete layers and continuous dynamics is not merely a mathematical curiosity; it is the blueprint for the next generation of adaptive architectures."

Research into the stability of these systems suggests that adding a diffusion term (SDEs) can further improve robustness against adversarial perturbations.
